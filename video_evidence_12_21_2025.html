Observations for Professional Review

Contact/Placement Sensitivity (1:13:11 - 1:19:57)

The floor-contact vs. lifted-device comparison is suggestive of vibration-coupled transmission. However, consumer microphones and phone/tablet housings can also change response depending on surface coupling. A professional assessment (including vibration measurement and calibrated spectrum analysis) is recommended to confirm mechanism and source.

  • When device placed on floor: tonal band appears to increase in relative intensity
  • When lifted off floor: tonal band decreases
  • When placed on printer drawer (elevated surface): SPL meter readings trend toward mid-40s dB(C)
  • When placed back on floor: tonal band returns quickly

This repeated pattern is documented as a potential indicator of structure-coupled transmission for expert evaluation.

Working Hypothesis

One working hypothesis is that structural or air-gap transmission pathways may exist at shared boundaries (e.g., wall/floor junctions, penetrations, ducts, cavities). Confirmatory inspection and testing is required before attributing cause.

A persistent low-frequency tonal component near ~59‚Äì60 Hz was observed starting at 1:03:05. It does not appear to follow a musical rhythm. Possible sources for expert consideration:
