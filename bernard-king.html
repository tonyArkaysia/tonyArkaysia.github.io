Decoding Bernard King’s Tactical & Analytical Prowess in Scoring Efficiency

Bernard King, a legendary small forward in the NBA, strategically crafted his scoring approach and utilized precise positioning throughout his illustrious career. His insights into the game played a crucial role in his remarkable achievements.

Prime & Efficiency

Across 14 seasons, with peak stretches at Golden State (GSW) and New York (NYK), King showcased elite efficiency. With GSW he posted a .577 FG%; with NYK he maintained .543 FG% — rare numbers for a 6'7" wing operating from multiple levels.

All-Time Context

Among players with 2,000+ field goals made, King sits among the top career field-goal marks historically. That standing, paired with high-usage, small-forward responsibilities, underlines his shot selection and footwork economy.

Volume Scoring

Beyond percentages, King delivered monster nights throughout the late 80s and early 90s, recording multiple 50-point games — capped by a signature 60-point eruption — validating sustainable creation, not just hot shooting.

Why It Travels

King’s mix of angle control, shoulder feints, and pace variance created repeatable looks. It’s a blueprint for modern wings: win the space first, then simplify the finish.
