Acoustic Trauma & Noise Nuisance – Legal Chronology

2006–2018 – Excessive Sound Levels

Church buys the property and gradually increases noise levels over the years. Starts with quiet prayer sessions → adds choir → microphone & speakers → live band with full instrumentals and concert stage (drums, guitar, piano).

April 2021 – Post-COVID Escalation

I return home to care for my elderly parents and notice a major escalation. Sessions now last 4–6 hours almost daily, disrupting health and well-being. Music sessions feature drum-dominant songs, akin to rock music. Father recovering from serious lung illness; I’m transitioning careers into data programming work. I verbally notify my landlord, who acknowledges the problem but takes no direct action.

2021 – NYPD Warnings & Sewing Machine Issue

Multiple NYPD complaints filed; noise levels repeatedly exceed legal limits (Exhibits i3 & i4). July 2021: landlord’s sewing machine use increases in daytime. When I raise the issue, landlord deflects. I temporarily set it aside to focus on church noise.

October 2021 – DOB Violations

Contact DOB for possible illegal conversion. Partial Vacate Order issued on the church for unrelated violations; they remain closed for approx. one year. (NYC Buildings record)

2023 – Issue Resumes & Escalation

Church reopens; noise resumes at full volume. Multiple verbal warnings issued to Pastor, his son, and brother-in-law (Exhibits i1 & i2). Multiple 311 complaints filed. NYPD warnings by Officers Motato and Fuentes (Exhibits i11). DEP faulty inspection claims no jurisdiction. MEND Mediation fails (Exhibit i6). Legal notice sent, signed by me, my uncle, and my mother. Noise worsens with overlap of sewing machine, bar, and church. Begin tinnitus attacks, medical testing, and purchase of sound meters and recording equipment (advised by David Smith of Sound Testimony).

March 2024 – DEP Re-Contacted

Requested second inspection; DEP finds excessive noise and issues fines (Exhibit i5). Church continues noise before soundproofing is installed.

May 2024 – Faulty Soundproofing

Church installs improper soundproofing. Structure resonates with low-frequency vibrations. Landlord notified of hearing/health issues; no action taken. Landlord denies vibration issues.

June–July 2024 – Witness Reports

Symptoms worsen. DEP refuses new inspection until September. Low frequencies suspected from heavy machinery. Landlord notified again of my diagnosed permanent hearing condition; no action taken.

Aug–Sept 2024 – Seeking Relief & Medical Assistance

I drive to remote areas for relief. Symptoms worsen: headaches, tinnitus, shortness of breath. Doctors confirm permanent condition. Notice church staff turn off HVAC after I bang on wall.

October 2024 – Medical & Soundproofing Adjustments

Consult with Dr. Adams (tinnitus specialist) who refers me to Dr. Natan Bauman. Church reconstructs wall; faulty triple-leaf effect suspected. Adjustments lessen but do not remove vibrations.

November 2024 – Dr. Bauman Specialist Evaluation

Initial evaluation confirms hyperacusis, tinnitus, and phonophobia. Treatment plan with therapy and custom devices. Follow-up set for Dec 31, 2024. Travel arranged to hotel with soundproofed room.

December 31, 2024 – Evaluation & Healing Therapy

Dr. Bauman conducts advanced tests; identifies octave band thresholds. Begins retraining therapy. States recovery is uncertain but possible for coping.

January 2025 – Retraining Therapy Effects

Temporary health improvements when away from home; symptoms return immediately upon return.

February 2025 – Environmental Changes

Church adjusts soundproofing; high-volume music reduced, but low-frequency resonance persists. Health somewhat improved. Began weekly therapy with Madeleine White. Retain lawyer for landlord notice.

Feb–May 2025 – Noise Still Relentless

Church sessions remain excessive, with abnormal low-frequency resonance. Bar continues despite warnings. Landlady still uses sewing machine daily despite notices. No landlord action taken.

July 2025 – HP Action Filed

After lawyer notice and landlord denial, proceed with HP Action. July 22: HPD inspection retains my statement.

August 11, 2025 – Housing Court Date

Case scheduled to address hazardous conditions, landlord inaction, and request for relief.
